Islamic studies ( Beginner to Advanced )
Duration: Every Day 25-30 Minutes

Course Content

Foundational Knowledge ?

This course offers a comprehensive introduction to Islamic theology, jurisprudence, history, and ethics, providing students with a solid understanding of the foundational principles and concepts of Islam.

Progressive Curriculum :

From beginner to advanced levels, students progress through a structured curriculum that covers a wide range of topics, including the Quran, Hadith, Fiqh (Islamic jurisprudence), Seerah (Prophetic biography), and Islamic spirituality.

Critical Thinking and Analysis

Through engaging discussions, case studies, and research projects, students develop critical thinking skills and analytical abilities, enabling them to navigate contemporary issues and apply Islamic teachings to real-life situations.

Integration of Sources*:

Drawing from primary Islamic texts, scholarly works, and contemporary sources, students gain a holistic understanding of Islam, learning to interpret and contextualize religious teachings within historical, cultural, and social frameworks.
